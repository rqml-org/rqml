# RQML Trademark Policy (Draft)

The RQML name and logos help users identify software and documents that are compatible with the RQML standard.

This policy is intended to protect the community from confusion while allowing broad, fair use.

## Ownership

“RQML” and associated logos are trademarks (or intended trademarks) of the RQML project.

## Permitted use (no permission needed)

You may use “RQML” to:
- Refer to the RQML project, schema, or documentation
- Describe that your product supports or is compatible with RQML (truthfully)
- Link to this repository or to rqml.org
- Use RQML word marks in blog posts, talks, academic papers, and tutorials

Examples:
- “Validates RQML documents”
- “RQML schema support”
- “RQML-compatible editor”

## Restricted use (permission required)

You must obtain permission to:
- Use the RQML logo in a way that implies endorsement or official status
- Use “RQML” in your product or company name (e.g., “RQML Studio”) in a way that suggests it is official
- Claim or imply your implementation is “official”, “certified”, or “approved” by the RQML project
- Register domains or social handles that could confuse users (e.g., `rqml-official.example`)

## Compatibility and misleading claims

You may say “compatible with RQML” only if:
- Your product reads/writes documents that conform to a published RQML version, and
- Any limitations are clearly disclosed.

Do not use RQML branding to market incompatible formats as RQML.

## Forks and modified standards

If you fork or modify the schema in a way that breaks compatibility:
- Do not call the result “RQML” without clear qualifiers (e.g., “based on RQML”)
- Use a distinct name to avoid confusing users

## “Official” usage

The terms “official RQML schema”, “RQML standard”, and “rqml.org” refer to artifacts and releases published by this project.

## Contact

To request trademark permission, open an issue (or add a contact email once you have one).
